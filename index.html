What’s in a Pod?

Understanding Solid’s decentralized knowledge graphs

  • Ruben Verborgh
  • IDLab, Department of Electronics and Information Systems, Ghent University – imec

Abstract

The Solid ecosystem provides specifications for the publication and consumption of decentralized permissioned data, stored across multiple locations called Pods. A disconnect exists between the practical notion of a Pod and the protocol that provides access to its data, creating confusion as to what exactly a Pod is and how it relates to the technical specifications. To reinstate clarity, we examined possible interpretations of the technological underpinnings, and compared them from the viewpoints of storage, publication, and query. First, this article details the current understanding of Pods as a hierarchy of documents. Second, we propose a wider definition in which a Solid Pod is fundamentally a knowledge graph, which can be accessed through various Web APIs that act as views in a database sense. We argue that the latter zoomed-out perspective enables accessing and querying knowledge within Pods in more flexible and sustainable ways. These insights are crucial to reduce the dependency of Solid apps on concrete APIs and local assumptions about the shape and organization of data and the resulting performance. The suggested broader perspective supports Solid with evolving into a heterogeneous but interoperable ecosystem that accommodates for a multitude of data access patterns.
